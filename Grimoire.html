CODEX ARCANUM

Grimoire of Ancient Secrets

⚠ SOLEMN WARNING ⚠
These formulae are historical artifacts of immeasurable power.
Their reckless use may lead to madness, damnation, or worse.

Lux Aeterna

"Lux in tenebris lucet, et tenebrae eam non comprehenderunt."

Bougie vierge Essence de camphre Poudre de ver luisant

Origin: Manuscript from the Abbey of Saint Gregory, 13th century

Usage: Recite at midnight while holding a lit candle. The produced light is not physical but spiritual, revealing hidden truths.

Warning: Prolonged exposure may lead to disturbing visions of parallel realities.

Sigillum Protectionis

"Per haec sancta nomina: Tetragrammaton, Adonai, Emmanuel, Sabaoth, protege nos ab omni malo et insidia diaboli."

Craie consacrée Sel gemme Sang de colombe (optionnel)

Origin: Grimoire of Honorius, 15th century version

Usage: Draw the circle in the solar direction while pronouncing each word with conviction. Never break the circle before the ritual's end.

Warning: Incorrect pronunciation of sacred names may attract unwanted entities.

Evocatio Spiritum

"Spiritus sapientiae et intellectus, spiritus consilii et fortitudinis, spiritus scientiae et pietatis, adesto nobis."

Encens de myrrhe Miroir en obsidienne Eau bénite de source lunaire

Origin: Book of Abramelin, medieval translation

Usage: Perform only during the full moon. Prepare an invocation triangle and place the mirror at its center.

Warning: Never ask personal questions to invoked entities. Their knowledge exceeds our understanding.

Sanatio Arcana

"Per virtutem Dei vivi, percutio hanc infirmitatem, tero hanc corruptionem, expello hanc pestem in nomine Patris, et Filii, et Spiritus Sancti."

Herbe de Saint-Jean Ail sauvage Poudre d'émeraude

Origin: Manuscript from the Bibliothèque de l'Arsenal, Paris

Usage: Apply ingredients to affected area while reciting the formula. Repeat for three consecutive days.

Warning: This formula does not replace medical treatment. Serious illnesses require modern medical intervention.

Veritas Occulta

"Speculum veritatis, revela quod absconditum est. Ostende mihi quod scire desidero, sine fraude, sine dolo."

Miroir en argent Huile de lampe Plume de corbeau

Origin: Secret version of the Grimoire of Pope Honorius

Usage: Coat mirror with oil and write the question with the feather. Observe by candlelight between midnight and 3 AM.

Warning: The revealed truth may be difficult to accept. Never ask questions whose answers you don't wish to know.
